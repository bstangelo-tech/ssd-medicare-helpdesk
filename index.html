Enrollment Periods — 2026

Initial Enrollment Period (IEP)

7-month window: 3 months before + birthday month + 3 months after turning 65. No late enrollment penalty. Coverage start depends on which month they enroll. Best time to get Part A, B, and choose a plan.

Annual Enrollment Period (AEP)

Oct 15 – Dec 7, 2026 → Effective Jan 1, 2027
  • All Medicare beneficiaries may enroll in, switch, or drop MA/Part D plans
  • Peak agent selling season — SOA required 48 hrs in advance
  • Annual Notice of Change (ANOC) sent by Sept 30 — review with clients

Medicare Advantage Open Enrollment Period (OEP)

Jan 1 – Mar 31, 2026 → Effective 1st of following month
  • Current MA enrollees only — switch MA plans or return to Original Medicare + PDP
  • Cannot use to enroll in MA for the first time
  • One change allowed per OEP

General Enrollment Period (GEP)

Jan 1 – Mar 31 → Coverage starts July 1
  • For those who missed IEP — enroll in Part A and/or Part B
  • Late enrollment penalties may apply
  • May then enroll in MA plan or PDP during concurrent period

Special Enrollment Periods (SEPs)

  • Loss of employer/union coverage: 8-month SEP for Part B; 63-day SEP for Part D/MA
  • Move out of plan service area: 2 months before to 2 months after
  • Plan terminates or loses contract: 2-month SEP
  • Gain or lose Medicaid (Dual Eligible): Monthly SEP — no lock-in period
  • LIS/Extra Help determination: Quarterly SEP
  • Newly qualify for D-SNP, C-SNP, or I-SNP: Monthly continuous SEP
  • Enter or leave nursing home / LTC facility: Monthly SEP
  • 5-Star Plan SEP: Dec 8 – Nov 30 — switch to 5-star plan once per year
  • Exceptional circumstances: FEMA disaster, plan error, or CMS-approved event
  • PACE enrollment: Any time if available in area

Late Enrollment Penalties (LEP)

  • Part B: 10% of standard premium for each full 12-month period without coverage — permanent
  • Part D: 1% of national base beneficiary premium × number of months without creditable coverage — permanent and added to monthly premium
  • Part A: 10% premium penalty × 2 years of delay (applies only to those who must pay a premium)
Part D — 2026 Complete Reference
The IRA (Inflation Reduction Act) fully restructured Part D benefit design for 2026. This is one of the biggest talking points with clients this year.

2026 Part D Benefit Structure

  • Deductible phase: Max deductible $590 — many plans waive partially or fully
  • Initial coverage: Pay copays/coinsurance until $2,000 total OOP reached
  • Catastrophic coverage: After $2,000 OOP — $0 cost sharing for remainder of year
  • Coverage gap eliminated: No "donut hole" in 2026
  • Insulin: Max $35/mo per covered product; Part D deductible does NOT apply to insulin
  • Vaccines: $0 cost-sharing for all ACIP-recommended vaccines under Part D

Medicare Prescription Payment Plan (M3P)

  • Allows beneficiaries to spread annual OOP drug costs into equal monthly payments
  • Auto-renewal now active — 2025 enrollees automatically continue in 2026 unless they opt out
  • Available to all Part D enrollees (MA-PD and stand-alone PDP)
  • Agent tip: Ideal for clients on specialty or high-cost drugs — eliminates large January cost spikes

LIS / Extra Help — 2026

  • Full subsidy: Income ≤135% FPL + limited resources — $0 premium, deductible, very low copays
  • Partial subsidy: Income 135–150% FPL — reduced premiums and cost sharing
  • Auto-enrollment: SSA auto-enrolls eligible Medicaid/MSP recipients into a benchmark Part D plan
  • Quarterly SEP for LIS beneficiaries — can switch plans once per quarter (Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct)
  • Apply via: SSA at ssa.gov, call 1-800-772-1213, or via Social Security office
  • Wellcare and Centene received largest share of LIS auto-enrollment in 2026

Key Medigap Rules for Agents

  • Open Enrollment (OE): 6-month window starting when enrolled in Part B at age 65+ — guaranteed issue, no underwriting
  • Outside OE: Medical underwriting typically applies — carriers can decline or surcharge
  • Guaranteed Issue Rights: Available in specific situations (e.g., MA plan leaves area, employer coverage ends, plan becomes insolvent)
  • Birthday Rule: CA, ID, IL, KY, LA, MD, NV, OK, OR — allows switching to same or lesser plan on birthday without underwriting. Check state-specific rules.
  • No drug coverage: Medigap does NOT include Part D — clients must enroll in a separate PDP
  • No network: Any Medicare-accepting provider nationwide — ideal for travelers or those seeing many specialists
  • Community vs attained-age vs issue-age pricing: Understand which rating method carriers use in each state
  • Top carriers nationally: Mutual of Omaha, Aetna, UHC/AARP, Humana, Cigna/HealthSpring, BCBS affiliates, Colonial Penn, GPM Life, Transamerica, Globe Life
CMS Compliance — Agent Quick Reference
General reference only. Always consult your carrier's compliance department and the current CMS Medicare Communications and Marketing Guidelines (MCMG) for authoritative guidance.

Scope of Appointment (SOA)

  • Required before all MA/PDP marketing or sales appointments
  • Must be completed at least 48 hours before appointment — exceptions: walk-in events, beneficiary-initiated same-day contact
  • Only discuss plan types the beneficiary agreed to discuss on the SOA
  • Retain SOA documentation for 10 years
  • Accepted formats: paper, electronic signature, phone recording

TPMO Required Disclaimer (verbatim)

"We do not offer every plan available in your area. Any information we provide is limited to those plans we do offer in your area. Please contact Medicare.gov or 1-800-MEDICARE to get information on all of your options."
  • Must appear at the start of all marketing/sales calls
  • Required on all websites, print materials, and digital communications
  • Must disclose number of carriers represented
  • Call recording required for all marketing and enrollment calls

Prohibited Marketing Practices

  • No door-to-door solicitation without prior invitation from the beneficiary
  • No cold calling without prior express permission
  • No marketing in healthcare settings — exam rooms, waiting areas, pharmacy counters
  • No gifts or payments exceeding $15 per item or $75 aggregate per year to influence enrollment
  • No cash or cash-equivalent gifts (gift cards)
  • No misleading plan comparisons or selectively omitting unfavorable information
  • No marketing outside plan's CMS-approved service area
  • No implying government endorsement via use of Medicare name or CMS logos

New 2026 CMS Rule Changes

  • Plans cannot reverse a previously approved inpatient admission — except obvious error or fraud
  • MA appeals loopholes tightened — plans must honor prior authorization decisions
  • Insulin cost-sharing cap codified at ≤ $35/mo per covered product
  • Automatic election renewal for Medicare Prescription Payment Plan (M3P)
  • Enhanced Medicare Plan Finder — provider directories, more filters, new security features
  • CMS enhanced review of all MA marketing materials before distribution

Documentation Best Practices

  • Retain all SOA forms, call recordings, and enrollment documents for 10 years
  • Always confirm enrollment with carrier — get confirmation number
  • Verify Medicare Part A and B eligibility before every enrollment
  • For dual eligible clients — verify Medicaid status with state agency
  • Keep AHIP certification current — required annually before selling MA/Part D
  • Confirm carrier appointment is active in each state where you are writing business
  • Document all client disclosures, plan discussions, and material changes
